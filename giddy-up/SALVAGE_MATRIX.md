# Salvage Matrix

As of March 29, 2026.

This is the canonical cross-repo salvage view. The old invalid `Conker` scores are not reassigned as scores; they are decomposed into mechanisms and moved to the repo that can own them honestly.

| Historical branch | What it seemed to show | What actually survives | Reallocated to | Present form |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `Conker-5` | tiny learned structure can dominate causal scoring | structural control is unusually high leverage | `Conker` | legal controller research |
| `Conker-6` | near-perfect causal mask | bidirectional local determinism is real, but the score was illegal | `BLINX` first, then `giddy-up` | oracle probing, then causal proxy features |
| `Conker-7` | future-aware teacher produces a stronger student | training-time teacher signals can still matter | `giddy-up` | offline labels and bridge shaping |
| old tandem packing wins | tiny artifacts imply tiny honest models | packing and replay legality must be audited independently | audit layer | stricter packer and legality discipline |
| BLINX layered synthetic wins | shared structure can really be exploited when context families stay clean | noncausal recoverability is real but conditional | `BLINX` | layered codec and candidate-generation direction |

## Reallocation Rule

- `BLINX` owns the noncausal recoverability.
- `giddy-up` owns the translation into legal causal hints.
- `Conker` owns only the part that still survives under strict causal evaluation.

## What This Is Not

- not a scoreboard
- not a claim that invalid rows can be reused as frontier numbers
- not permission to let live bidirectional oracle scoring leak into causal eval
