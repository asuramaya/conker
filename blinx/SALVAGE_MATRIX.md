# Salvage Matrix

As of March 29, 2026.

This matrix is conceptual, not numerical. `BLINX` does not inherit invalid `Conker` scores; it inherits the part of those branches that actually reflected noncausal recoverability.

| Source signal | What it really meant | Why BLINX owns it | Present form |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `Conker-6` mask shock | many positions are almost determined by surrounding context | that is a bidirectional recoverability claim, not a legal causal score | oracle analysis and exact-reconstruction codec work |
| `Conker-7` teacher effect | future-aware supervision carries real structure | BLINX is the honest producer side for that discovery | offline labels and probe surfaces |
| old exact-match wins | local structure can be exploited much harder than expected | BLINX can test that without pretending it is causal | removal rounds, side-data accounting, codec falsification |
| synthetic layered wins in `BLINX-6/7` | reuse appears once context layers are cleanly separated | this is a codec and candidate-generation lesson | shared dictionaries and layered candidate design |

## What BLINX Keeps

- the noncausal recoverability lesson
- the idea that local structure can sometimes make symbols nearly deterministic
- the exact accounting discipline needed to tell whether that structure is actually compressible

## What BLINX Does Not Keep

- invalid `Conker` scores as if they were BLINX results
- causal-leaderboard claims
- any implication that BLINX results are automatically legal to reuse at eval time in `Conker`

## Reallocation Read

- `BLINX` discovers the structure.
- `giddy-up` translates the causal residue of that structure.
- `Conker` only consumes what still survives after causalization.
